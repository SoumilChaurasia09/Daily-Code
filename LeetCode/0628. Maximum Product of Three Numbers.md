# 0628. Maximum Product of Three Numbers

**Problem Name:** Maximum Product of Three Numbers  
**Difficulty:** Easy

---

## Question

Given an integer array `nums`, return the maximum product that can be obtained by multiplying any three numbers from the array.

---

## My Solution

```cpp
class Solution {
public:
    int maximumProduct(vector<int>& nums) {

        sort(nums.begin(), nums.end());

        int n = nums.size();

        int product1 = nums[n - 1] * nums[n - 2] * nums[n - 3];
        int product2 = nums[0] * nums[1] * nums[n - 1];

        return max(product1, product2);
    }
};
```

---

## Complexity

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Time Complexity** | `O(n log n)` |
| **Space Complexity** | `O(1)` *(Ignoring the space used by the sorting algorithm)* |

---

## What I Learned

This problem taught me that the maximum product is not always obtained by multiplying the three largest numbers.

If the array contains negative numbers, multiplying the two smallest (most negative) numbers can produce a large positive value. Therefore, I need to compare:

- The product of the three largest numbers.
- The product of the two smallest numbers and the largest number.

The larger of these two values is the answer.

---

## Personal Notes

At first, I assumed the answer would always come from the three largest elements after sorting the array. However, I realized that two negative numbers multiply to form a positive number, which can sometimes produce a larger product.

This problem reminded me to always consider edge cases involving negative numbers instead of relying only on the largest values.
